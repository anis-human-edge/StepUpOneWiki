# Distributed Publishing Model

StepUp.One's inbound strategy is not a marketing function -- it is a distributed publishing operation where every asset has a single human owner who is measured like a page founder.

## Core Formula

**Page + Person + Publishing Rhythm = Pipeline**

"One page, one owner, one publishing rhythm."

## Operating Model

Each team member owns a specific channel or landing page and is responsible for:

1. **Content creation** -- 3 pieces of good content daily (target: 1,000 pieces/year)
2. **Channel management** -- posting, engagement, and growth across their assigned channels
3. **Traffic measurement** -- using personalized UTM tracker links to measure which content drives landing page visits

## Metrics and Measurement

### Inbound Success Ladder

1. **Impressions** -- how many people see the content (current: 140,000/year; target: 1.4 million)
2. **Engagement** -- likes, comments, shares
3. **Click-through** -- the critical step: convincing someone to click a CTA and land on a dedicated page
4. **Landing page actions** (in order of value):
   1. Purchase (buy button)
   2. Book a call
   3. Register for communications (newsletter, updates)

### UTM Tracking

Every team member creates two personalized UTM tracker links:
- One for LinkedIn posts
- One for Twitter posts

This allows precise attribution: who is generating the most traffic, from which platform, from which post. Built using Google's Campaign URL Builder.

## The 13 Inbound Channels

The team identified 13 owned channels for inbound distribution: LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, newsletters, blogs, and 8 others. The current focus is on LinkedIn and Twitter, with video (TikTok, YouTube) as a growth area.

## Inbound Funnel

Five stages: **Discover -> Consume -> Evaluate -> Engage -> Convert**

The professional view uses these terms. The simple view (for Global South audiences) translates them: "Getting Found Online" instead of "Inbound Sales."
