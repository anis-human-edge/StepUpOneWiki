# Client Inbound Strategy

Inbound is the counterpart to outbound: **make the clients come to you through content.** You create content, let the audience come to you one-to-many, with unnamed audience, public content, views, engagement, DMs, and booked calls.

## How Inbound Differs from Outbound

| Dimension | Outbound | Inbound |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Target | You pick a person | You pick the content |
| Direction | You go to them | They come to you |
| Scale | One-to-one | One-to-many |
| Audience | Named, specific | Unnamed, broad |
| Channel | Private message | Public content |
| Outcome | Reply or no reply | Views, engagement, DMs, booked calls |
| Proof metric | Opportunities | Leads (impressions -> leads -> meetings -> wins) |

The priorities remain the same: win new, serve current, grow existing, win back. But the proof metric changes: outbound tracks **opportunities**, inbound tracks **leads**. A lead is someone who came to you after seeing your content. An opportunity is something you created by reaching out.

## Inbound as a Portfolio of Strategies

A critical architectural decision: inbound cannot be forced into the same six-step pipeline as outbound. Outbound has one job (find a person, reach them). Inbound is a portfolio of parallel strategies that run simultaneously:

### Strategy 1: Commenting
The most powerful inbound strategy discovered so far. One well-timed, well-placed comment on a trending post can outperform a thousand self-promotional posts.

**Why commenting works:**
- Timing: the comment appears when the right people are already reading about the right topic
- Credibility: you're responding to someone else's analysis, not promoting yourself
- Reach: comments on trending posts get thousands of impressions (one comment got 834 impressions on a non-critical news comment; impactful comments reach 10,000+)
- No production needed: no image, no copy, no generate -- just identify the right post and write the comment

**Example comment:** "What a brilliant analysis of founders raising capital. We have also come to the same conclusion after working with hundreds of founders raising their pre-seed. We believe success is a function of building and running systems. To learn more, go to [link]."

**The research engine for commenting:**
- **Source 1: Watch list** -- 100 to 300 segmented accounts (not the previously mentioned 30-50), checked multiple times daily
- **Source 2: Trending topics** -- LinkedIn topic searches, news sources in the target category
- **Source 3: Competitor posts** -- monitoring competitor activity for commenting opportunities
- **Source 4: Search intent** -- identifying what target audiences are searching for
- **Daily output:** ranked list of 20-30 comment opportunities. Team leader picks top 3-5 for execution.

### Strategy 2: Posting
Content creation for LinkedIn and other platforms. Sub-types include text posts, image posts, video posts, carousels, polls.

Key lesson: the team's existing posts on the fundraising page look promotional. "Nobody respects you for the post you're creating. It looks like you're just trying to say big things about yourself." Content must provide advice and value, not promote the company.

### Strategy 3: Blogging
Long-form content targeting Google search. Different audience, different rhythm, different ICP targeting.

### Strategy 4: Newsletter
Regular email content to subscribers. Separate strategy from posting with its own logic.

### Strategy 5+: Profile Transformation, Podcasts, Video, etc.
Additional strategies emerge over time. A client does not have to run all strategies -- they pick which ones matter. A smaller client might run 2-3; a bigger client could run all of them.

## The Core Insight: Research is Everything

"Each inbound strategy's value is almost entirely determined by the quality of the research that picks the signal. Execution is the easy part. Research is where 10x comes from."

The research engine is the bottleneck -- not the ability to write comments or posts, but the ability to identify the right post, the right moment, the right audience. This is what generates exponential returns.

## Funnel Metrics

The inbound funnel:
1. **Impressions** -- how many people saw the content
2. **Leads** -- how many people raised their hand (DM'd, commented, clicked CTA)
3. **Meetings** -- leads that converted to scheduled conversations
4. **Wins** -- meetings that converted to paying clients

The bottom half of the funnel (meetings to wins) is identical to outbound. The top half (impressions to leads) is unique to inbound.

## PRD Development

The inbound strategy is being translated into a Product Requirements Document (PRD) for the inbound system, following the same pattern used for the outbound PRD. Each strategy will have its own section with:
- ICP targeting
- Content strategy
- Research methodology
- Execution playbook
- Proof metrics
