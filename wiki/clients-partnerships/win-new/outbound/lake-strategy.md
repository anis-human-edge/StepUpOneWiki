# Lake Strategy

Accelerator-based prospecting framework where "lakes" are accelerators/incubators and "fish" are founders.

## Core Concepts

- **Lake** = an accelerator, incubator, or investor firm (YC, Techstars, Plug and Play, etc.)
- **Fish** = founders associated with that lake
- **Two types of fish:**
  1. Founders who have already received funding from the lake (inside the lake)
  2. Founders who are actively trying to enter the lake (outside the lake, pitching)

## Strategy by Fish Type

### Founders Inside the Lake (Already Funded)

Standard fundraising outreach does not work for these founders -- they are "already dressed up for money" and focused on building, marketing, and product. The approach must shift:

- **Angle of attack:** "You have been given a lot of strategy; how many of them are you really executing every day?" -- an execution-focused message that resonates because most founders struggle to act on all the advice they receive
- **Non-outreach delivery:** Cold emails and LinkedIn campaigns do not work for top-tier lake founders (e.g., YC). The team must find creative, relationship-based ways to deliver the message (e.g., engaging with their content on Twitter, attending events)

### Founders Outside the Lake (Seeking Entry)

The general "we are here to help first-time founders" strategy remains effective for founders trying to enter accelerators.

## Lake Quality Ranking

Not all lakes are equal. Quality hierarchy discussed in sessions:

1. **Y Combinator** -- largest (13,000+ founders in database), highest quality, but hardest to reach. ~500 new founders per batch.
2. **Plug and Play** -- smaller lake but "advertises their fish," making it easy to connect within minutes
3. **Movement 51** -- non-profit focused on female entrepreneurs; lower quality founders on average, but potential for partnership with the incubator itself

## Strategic Insight

For lower-quality lakes (e.g., Movement 51), the better play is to connect with the incubator's founders/operators and offer to help their top 10% of founders with high-quality fundraising, rather than hunting individual founders.

## Metrics

The key question per lake: how many fish are inside, and how many are actively trying to enter? This determines which messaging angle to use.
