# Client Outbound Strategy

Outbound is StepUp.One's primary revenue engine. The definition is simple: **go and get the clients by messaging.** You pick a specific person, reach out one-to-one with a private message, and the outcome is either a reply or no reply.

## The Four Client Motions

All go-to-market activity -- inbound and outbound -- falls into four priorities:

1. **Win new clients** -- the primary focus
2. **Serve current clients** -- ongoing delivery and relationship management
3. **Grow existing clients** -- selling additional services (e.g., adding fundraising to a GTM engagement)
4. **Win back lost clients** -- re-engaging clients who left, using new results and systems as proof

Each motion applies to both inbound and outbound. A single client can have multiple mutually exclusive opportunities tracked across these motions. The client (e.g., "Marson") remains one entity, but separate opportunity records track different objectives.

## Proof Metrics

Three core metrics prove outbound success:

1. **Number of opportunities** -- how many outbound conversations have been initiated
2. **Number of meetings** -- how many of those converted to scheduled calls
3. **Number of wins** -- how many became paying clients

The team debated whether the monthly message target (400 messages) should be prioritized over meetings. Mohamed Anis was unambiguous: meetings matter more than message count. Focusing on hitting 400 messages at the expense of meeting quality is "extremely stupid."

## The Win New Client System (9 Steps)

The outbound system is a sequential pipeline of nine steps. The first six are executed by the team leader; the last three by the team member.

### Team Leader Steps (Research & Preparation)

**Step 1: Know Your Client as a Person (KYCP)**
- Who is this person? Time zone, passions, vision, family, country.
- Inputs: LinkedIn profile, social media, conversation transcripts.
- Output: KYCP document.

**Step 2: Know Your Client's Business (KYCB)**
- What does the company do? Products, pricing, geography, revenue, team, mission.
- Inputs: everything from Step 1 + KYCP document.
- Output: KYCB document.

**Step 3: Client Context, Needs, and Timing (CNT)**
- What are the client's unspoken, desperate needs? What's buried in conversations?
- This is the most nuanced step -- it requires reading between the lines.
- Inputs: everything from Steps 1-2 + both documents.
- Output: CNT document.

**Step 4: Know Your Client's Target Audience (KYCTA)**
- Who is the client's ICP? Universal list of all ICPs, categorized into segments, prioritized with reasoning.
- This is where human intelligence is critical -- AI gets target audience wrong 90% of the time without specific human direction.
- Inputs: all previous documents.
- Output: KYCTA document.

**Step 5: Where to Find the ICPs**
- Where are the target accounts active? LinkedIn, events, communities, publications.

**Step 6: Timing**
- When to talk to them. May be combined with Step 5 into a single timing-and-location strategy.

### Team Member Steps (Execution)

**Step 7: Messaging Strategy**
- Connecting, engaging, educating, reaching, and closing clients.
- Includes follow-up and close as part of the overall flow.

**Steps 8-9: Execution and Close**
- Send messages, handle responses, book meetings, close deals.

Each step is dependent on the previous document -- this is by design, preventing shortcuts. The system is universal: the same nine steps apply to every client, though the content differs.

## AI Integration

The system uses AI at two cost tiers:

- **Premium AI ($20/client)**: "Extremely high class" research output, deep nuance and depth. Used for high-value clients where the deal justifies the cost.
- **Economy AI ($1/client)**: Good enough, usable, but different in quality and nuance. Used for initial research and high-volume prospecting.

Strategy: use expensive AI for advice and planning, cheap AI for data collection and execution.

The AI generates comprehensive client profiles that team leaders validate. These documents are not secret -- they are shared directly with clients to confirm details, target audience, and needs. The purpose is validation, not espionage.

## Territory and Targeting

- The team shifted from targeting **founders** to **investors** for a two-month period. Investor targeting produced significantly fewer meetings, as predicted.
- Decision: revert to founders by end of April 2026, or pivot to a completely different group (executives).
- Farah Ibrahim confirmed that founder-focused campaigns previously generated many meetings.
