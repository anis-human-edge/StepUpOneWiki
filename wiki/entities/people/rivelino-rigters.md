# Rivelino "Blackrockstar" Rigters

Community leader and foundation chairman based in Amsterdam, Netherlands. Works with underserved youth, including people inside and post-release from prison, refugees, and trauma-affected young people.

## Background

- **Location:** Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **Organization:** Runs a foundation focused on youth development and reintegration
- **Foundation Chairman:** Matias, a tech entrepreneur who (with his brother) invented a solar-powered speaker and sold it to Sonos for $100 million
- **Family:** Has a son, turning 5 in July 2026

## Work with Underserved Populations

Rivelino works with several populations:

- **Youth inside prison** -- provides workshops, assigns small tasks, tries to maintain engagement. Limited by Netherlands regulations: prisoners do not have full internet access due to safety and privacy rules.
- **Post-release youth** -- the primary group with potential for StepUp.One collaboration. These individuals have internet access and need employable skills.
- **Refugees** -- including a highly motivated refugee who is a talented singer, gives free swimming lessons, attends school despite lacking immigration papers, records songs, speaks at congresses, and earns good money.
- **Trauma-affected youth** -- people "broken by life, circumstances, or mental issues" who struggle with motivation and appointments. Some have been repeatedly signed up for talent programs but fail to show up.

## Philosophy

"I don't want to just bring them money. I want to help them discover their god-given talent so they can make a living." He references the "teach a man to fish" principle. His challenge is maintaining engagement with people who have given up on themselves or have been given up on by others.

## Collaboration with StepUp.One

Met with Mohamed Anis on Apr 13, 2026 (evening session). Key discussion points:

- **Target group:** Post-release population is the best fit for StepUp.One's AI curriculum, since prison populations lack internet access.
- **Proposed next steps:** Meet with the youngsters, discuss what skills they want to learn; potentially organize a "show and tell" workshop where StepUp.One demonstrates AI possibilities.
- **Speaker refurbishment project:** Rivelino described an existing project where youth install Bluetooth plates in old speakers. Mohamed Anis advised against it -- "a rich man's idea" that cannot compete with Chinese mass production and doesn't scale to help thousands.
- **Music connection:** Many of Rivelino's youth have musical talent. AI music creation could be a bridge skill, though the path to revenue needs to be clearer.
