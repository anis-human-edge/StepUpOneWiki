# Fundraising Engine

Full-cycle investor outreach operation for founders. StepUp.One runs the entire pipeline daily on behalf of the founder -- strategy, targeting, messaging, AI digital twin, and a dedicated execution team.

**Audience:** Founders (pre-seed, seed, first-time raisers, solo operators, technical builders)
**Pricing:** $400/month per engine, cancel anytime, no contracts, no setup fees
**URL:** https://stepup.one/founder-fundraising

## Core Thesis

Cold outreach is dead. Contextual penetration is everything. Founders start fundraising when they need money, but by then it is too late -- investor relationships take 6-12 months to build. Cold pitches convert at less than 2%. The engine builds the foundation first, then runs daily execution.

## How It Works: Two Layers

### Layer 1 -- Build the Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

1. **Fundraising Strategy** -- Define stage, amount, ideal investor profile. Choose engine type (Relationship, Intent, or Signal Led).
2. **Investor Targeting** -- Fund-level (stage focus, cheque size, thesis), partner-level (who leads deals in the niche), timing-level (new funds, deployment signals). Every investor scored before entering the pipeline. Not a bulk Crunchbase scrape.
3. **Profile Transformation** -- Rebuild LinkedIn: headline signals traction and category, about section tells the founder story, featured section shows pitch materials and early wins.
4. **Messaging Architecture** -- Multi-angle sequences: connect (reference portfolio/thesis), educate (share traction/insights), pitch (tailored to exact thesis). A/B tested across 5 angles, optimised weekly.
5. **AI Digital Twin** -- 100% brand safety with zero hallucinations. Human-in-the-loop: operators audit every word. AI generates drafts based strictly on pitch deck. Nothing fires without strict approval routing.
6. **Team Deployment** -- Strategist (owns fundraising plan), outreach specialist (runs daily cycle), content & AI (manages twin and pipeline). The founder does not manage them.

### Layer 2 -- Run the Engine (Week 3+)

| Phase | Action | Principle |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| P1: Connect | Build recognition -- follow, like, comment with specific POV. By the time the connection request lands, the investor has seen the founder's name 3-5 times. | Recognition before permission |
| P2: Engage | Daily engagement whether investors posted or not. Extract live vectors (fund announcements, podcast transcripts). Inject founder perspective into allocator discussions. React in real-time to market catalysts. | Familiarity before pitch |
| P3: Pitch | Only after recognition + familiarity. Educate with traction updates, then pitch referencing specific thesis and portfolio. Book meetings. | Context before ask |
| P4: Nurture | Post on founder's behalf, follow up on structured cadence, update pipeline, flag warm investors. Non-converters loop back to Engage. | Protect the pipeline |

## Three Engine Types

| Engine | Volume | Best For | Approach |
|--------|--------|----------|----------|
| **Relationship Led** | 200 investors/month | Founders planning 6-12 months ahead | Long build, high trust, full warm-up sequence |
| **Intent Led** | 100 investors/month | Founders with traction | Target investors who have backed the same niche; past behavior predicts future investment |
| **Signal Led** | 100 investors/month | Founders who need capital now | Monitor real-time signals (posts, fund announcements); engage within 24 hours |

Founders can run one engine or all three in parallel. The Execution Strategist selects the engine geometry during onboarding.
