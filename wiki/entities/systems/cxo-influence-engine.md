# CXO Influence Engine

Executive authority-to-influence conversion system. Runs an entire executive influence operation across three dimensions: external market, internal organization, and personal network. Not personal branding -- operationalized authority that converts into revenue, deals, talent, and legacy.

**Audience:** CEOs, CXOs, founders needing visibility with investors/clients/talent, senior leaders targeting board roles or speaking engagements
**Pricing:** $1,000/month starting per engine, structured as 12-month partnership. 4-week foundation phase before daily execution begins.
**URL:** https://stepup.one/cxo-influencer

## Core Thesis

Most executives have authority but cannot convert it into influence that moves numbers. High visibility but low resonance. Strategic directives face friction. Dormant relationships. The expertise never reaches the right people because the executive lacks time to systematically distribute it. Without a system, they rely on chance.

## Three Influence Dimensions

| Dimension | Scope | Examples |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| **External** | Market narrative | Peers, analysts, media, board connections |
| **Internal** | Organisational velocity | Internal leaders, board members |
| **Personal** | Network activation | Dormant relationships, partners, talent |

## How It Works: Two Layers

### Layer 1 -- Build the Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

1. **Influence Strategy** -- Map three influence dimensions. Define what the executive must be known for.
2. **Stakeholder Targeting** -- Stakeholder by stakeholder. Market (peers, analysts, media), org (internal leaders, board), network (dormant relationships, partners). Each scored on influence value.
3. **Profile Transformation** -- Rebuild headline and banner, rewrite about section as category voice, curate featured content to position expertise.
4. **Messaging Architecture** -- External (market commentary, contrarian takes), internal (strategic narratives), personal (messages opening new doors). A/B tested, optimised weekly.
5. **AI Digital Twin** -- Trained on leadership voice. Ingests organisational context. Learns unique communication style. Writes newsletters, generates posts, comments, and messages. Uses RAG to construct asymmetric, contrarian takes derived from the executive's internal Slack/Notion docs.
6. **Team Deployment** -- Strategist (influence positioning), comms specialist (executive correspondence), content (narrative timeline).

### Layer 2 -- Run the Engine (Week 5+)

| Phase | Action | Principle |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| P1: Connect | Follow targets, like posts, comment with specific POV, connect. Name is familiar before appearing in feed. | Recognition before permission |
| P2: Engage | Publish thought leadership posts. Engage on trending industry conversations. Publish influencer newsletters. Daily engagement whether stakeholders posted or not. | Authority before opportunity |
| P3: Pitch | Not a sales message -- a positioning move. Surface board opportunities through visible expertise. Attract talent through employer authority. Warm investor relationships through market credibility. | "I keep seeing their name" |
| P4: Nurture | Track indices: Relationship Index (right people engaging?), Engagement Index (reaching decision-makers?), Influencer Index (ranking vs. peers?). Adjust weekly. | Consistency before virality |

## Three Core Principles

1. **Recognition before Permission** -- They must see your name repeatedly before you ask for attention
2. **Authority before Opportunity** -- Position, don't pitch. Surface opportunities organically through undeniable expertise
3. **Consistency before Virality** -- Daily systematic engagement beats occasional viral spikes. Influence compounds through reliable presence.

## Key Differentiator

Not "hustle bro" LinkedIn ghostwriting. Highly technical, nuance-driven intelligence campaigns. The AI Digital Twin replicates the executive's exact syntax, flaws, and cadences -- trained on previous writing, podcast transcripts, and memos. Zero hallucinations. The executive looks like a category architect, not an influencer.
