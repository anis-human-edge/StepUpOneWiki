# Go-To-Market Engine

Full client acquisition system for founders and service sellers. StepUp.One finds the right buyers, builds relationships, manages conversations, and books meetings. The founder/seller handles the call.

**Audience:** Founders, business owners selling B2B services ($5,000+), solo operators
**Pricing:** $400/month per system, cancel anytime, no contracts, no setup fees
**URL:** https://stepup.one/founder-gtm

## Core Thesis

Nobody replies to strangers. You must build trust before asking for anything. Sales done properly takes 3-4 hours per day, every day, for months. Most founders and service sellers cannot sustain this while building product or delivering services.

## Who This Is For

- **People selling services** -- B2B business owners running out of time
- **Founders who know their buyers** -- but cannot spend 3-4 hours daily on outreach
- **Sellers of expensive services** ($5,000+) requiring trust before purchase
- **People with good ideas** but no time to write LinkedIn/Facebook posts daily
- **Those wanting long-term success** -- a system that builds steady income, not spam

## How It Works: Two Steps

### Step 1 -- Set Up Work (Weeks 1-2)

1. **Who to Sell To** -- Define what you sell, what problem it fixes, why they need it now
2. **Finding the Buyer** -- Company (big/small, country), person (boss? active poster?), timing (new job?)
3. **Fixing Your Profile** -- Title shows what you fix (not job title), about section shows helpfulness, proof of good work
4. **What to Say** -- Exact templates: connect (short, friendly, no selling), talk (reply to posts for recognition), offer (polite when ready)
5. **AI Computer Helper** -- Software that reads how you type, uses your words, generates comments on posts instantly
6. **Support Team** -- Coach (plans targets), helper (daily clicking/reading), trainer (better messages)

### Step 2 -- Start Talking (Week 3+)

| Phase | Action | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1. Find | Follow, like 3 posts over days, write a comment, then connect | Make them know you |
| 2. Talk | Comment helpfully on their problems, congratulate job changes, engage in their industry | Build a friendship |
| 3. Ask | Send a teaching message (not begging), use their name and company, ask for 15-min call | Offer to help |
| 4. Stay in touch | Post on your profile, check back every few weeks, keep notes on what they said | Don't forget them |

## Three Ways to Find Clients

| Method | Volume | Best For | Approach |
|--------|--------|----------|----------|
| **Building Friendships Over Time** | 200 people/month | No immediate urgency, long-term relationships | Slowly build trust via likes, careful comments, offer service when they feel they know you |
| **Finding People Who Are Buying Now** | 100 people/month | Clear service, known buyer | Target leaders who recently bought similar services; past spending predicts future buying |
| **Watching for Sudden Changes** | 100 people/month | Need clients when problems appear | Monitor job changes, promotions, problem posts; message within 24 hours using their exact words |

## Key Differentiator

Every message sounds like a real person. The buyer thinks they are talking to the seller, not a robot. Computer automation handles the boring work (finding names, typing fast, tracking replies). The human handles the important work (showing respect, building trust, having real conversations).
