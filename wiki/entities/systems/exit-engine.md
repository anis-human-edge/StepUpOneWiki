# M&A / Exit Engine

Covert M&A sourcing for portfolio companies that will not hit venture scale but have recoverable value. StepUp.One deploys outbound engines to map PE firms, strategic competitors, and acqui-hirers to land founders safely and recover fund IRR.

**Audience:** VCs, GPs, fund managers with portfolio companies that have stalled
**Pricing:** $3,000-$5,000/month + 1-3% success fee on finalized deal value
**URL:** https://stepup.one/investors/exit-engine

## The Problem: The Liquidity Paradox

When a portfolio company stalls, most GPs write it off as a mathematical certainty of venture capital. But there is often $2M-$10M of recoverable value sitting on the balance sheet. 30% of write-offs contain recoverable IP, teams, or customer bases that strategic buyers actively want.

## How It Works

All outreach is strictly covert and thesis-led. The asset is not named initially. StepUp.One probes strategic intent and only executes NDAs when explicit buyer interest in the vertical is confirmed.

### Three Execution Pathways

| Pathway | Description | Target Buyers |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **PATH-01: Strategic Acquisition** | Sourcing buyers in identical or synergic market verticals | Competitors looking to eliminate competition or absorb market share |
| **PATH-02: Talent Acqui-hire** | Finding companies aggressively recruiting who want to acquire the engineering unit intact | Technology companies wanting teams without external recruiting fees |
| **PATH-03: Asset / IP Sale** | Selling underlying technology to organizations with outdated infrastructure | Legacy organizations needing the technology minus brand and admin overhead |

## Key Differentiator

Covert, thesis-led outreach. StepUp.One does not manage founder psychology (that is the board's domain) but arms the GP with specific, market-validated acquisition pathways to present as a viable alternative to zero.
