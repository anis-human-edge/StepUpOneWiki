# Daily Learning Session Operations

StepUp.One runs multiple Daily Learning sessions per day. These sessions are the heartbeat of the organization -- where training, client work, strategy development, and team coordination happen simultaneously.

## Schedule

Sessions run at multiple times throughout the day to accommodate different time zones and participant availability. Known session times from Apr 13-15, 2026:

- **Morning (07:51 BST)** -- primary session, typically led by Mohamed Anis
- **Midday (12:50 BST)** -- continuation session, often picks up where morning left off
- **Evening (18:51 BST)** -- used for external calls (e.g., with Rivelino Rigters in the Netherlands)

When Mohamed Anis is unavailable (e.g., London client meeting on Apr 15), senior team members like [Farah Ibrahim](../entities/people/farah-ibrahim.md) lead the session.

## Time Allocation Debate

Farah Ibrahim raised a concern on Apr 15: the 11-12 meeting block was being used for system building rather than client work. His position: "Whatever you are doing is not adding any value to our clients." Mohamed Anis agreed to separate system building from client-facing work.

## Beginner Support Protocol

Identified problem: beginners waste session time by needing fundamental instruction. Solution: dedicated person assists beginners in a separate, short time frame. The main session continues uninterrupted.

## Two Core Sessions (System 1 and System 2)

As of April 2026, the daily sessions are structured around two non-negotiable core systems:

### System 1: Winning New Clients (10:00-11:00 AM)

Led by Ahmed Farhan with Patience Ciza and Mohamed sharmuge Mohamed. Covers:
- Reviewing client opportunities and pipeline stages
- Follow-up strategy and execution
- Client vetting and qualification
- Teaching graduates how to identify and cook opportunities

This system has proven effective: 1,400 opportunities built in the CRM over ~3 months. Key personnel have become "masters" of messaging and client outreach.

### System 2: Working with Clients (11:00 AM+)

Led by [Nuuh Iftin](../entities/people/nuuh-iftin.md). Covers:
- Existing client outcomes and delivery
- Problem identification and escalation
- CRM task tracking for client work

System 2 is being built from scratch with the objective that "no one is allowed to fail" -- neither client nor team member. The system must guide, teach, and add value. Team leaders present problems; the session solves them collaboratively.

### Session Restructuring (April 2026)

The core session time shifted from 10:00 AM to 11:00 AM to avoid conflicts with other sessions, then back to 10:00 AM when Mohamed Anis insisted Priority 1 (client work) must come first. The "winning clients" session was consolidated into a single 10:00-11:00 AM slot under Ahmed Farhan's leadership.

## Meeting Culture and Discipline

Key cultural issues addressed in sessions:

- **No dependency on leader announcements:** Team members were waiting for Sharmug or Ahmed Farhan to announce the daily meeting before joining. The mandate: anyone should start the meeting, share their screen, and begin work even if they are the only person present.
- **No parallel meetings:** Internal meetings must not overlap with the mandatory team meeting.
- **Camera on policy:** Anyone joining a client meeting must have good lighting and keep their camera on for the entire duration.
- **Review, not do:** The primary purpose of sessions should be to review work already completed, not to perform tasks like generating content live.

## Teaching Approach (Graduate Development)

Mohamed Anis established a layered teaching approach for developing graduates:

- **Graduates must talk:** If a graduate keeps quiet while their opportunity is discussed, do not continue cooking their food. They must explain why they think a client is good or bad.
- **One-on-one focus:** Work with one graduate at a time rather than waiting for large groups. "Just work with Gabriel. Make him intelligent."
- **Early bird priority:** Graduates who arrive at 10:00 AM get help. Latecomers who join at 11:30 are told to come back tomorrow.
- **Pressure cooking:** Graduates handle the initial stages (educate, convince), then bring "cooked" opportunities to Mohamed Anis for pressure cooking (final close).
