# Organizational Priorities

Four ranked priorities that govern how StepUp.One allocates time, money, and brains. Defined across multiple Daily Learning sessions in April 2026.

## The Four Priorities

| Priority | Description | Leader |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| 1. Work with clients | Deliver outcomes for existing, paying clients | Nuuh Iftin |
| 2. Win new clients | Outbound + inbound to acquire new clients | Ahmed Farhan |
| 3. Expand current clients | Generate expansion ideas and proposals for existing clients | Ahmed Farhan |
| 4. Win back lost clients | Re-engage cancelled/lost clients by continuing to provide value | Abdulkadir Mhina (proposed) |

## Key Principles

**Priority 1 is the hardest thing to do every day.** Mohamed Anis identified that the team gravitates toward easy tasks (like sending 20 connections) and avoids the difficult work of meaningful daily client discussion. Yet Priority 1 is the only activity that directly generates revenue.

**No campaign starts before payment is received.** This rule was established after the Technocon incident: a client went bankrupt, their bank retrieved the payment, and StepUp.One was charged a $100 dispute fee on top of losing the revenue.

**Expansion is separate from delivery.** The team debated whether Priority 3 should be folded into Priority 1. Mohamed Anis kept them separate because teams focused on daily delivery neglect expansion opportunities. The expansion leader generates ideas and proposals without being the primary account manager.

**Win-back requires a strategy, not just hope.** Abdulkadir Mhina proposed Priority 4, arguing that ending the relationship when a deal is cancelled wastes the trust already built. Continue providing value -- a fundraising client who cancels may later need GTM.

## Session Structure (System 1 and System 2)

The priorities map to two core daily sessions:

- **System 1: Winning new clients** (10:00-11:00 AM) -- led by Ahmed Farhan with Patience Ciza and Mohamed sharmuge Mohamed
- **System 2: Working with clients** (11:00 AM+) -- led by Nuuh Iftin, focused on client outcomes

System 1 has proven effective: 1,400 opportunities built in the CRM over ~3 months. System 2 is being built from scratch, with the objective that "no one is allowed to fail."

## Enforcement

- Each priority has a named leader accountable for results
- Financial compensation restructuring was discussed to incentivize priority work
- Meeting sessions are structured around priorities: client work first, then new business
