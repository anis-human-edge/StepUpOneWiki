### Details

- **Business Update**: Jason Malefakis's company (Wayo) has secured more working spaces and launched membership sales, creating recurring revenue. Jason is offloading operational tasks to concentrate on fundraising.

- **Product Definition and Investment Pitch**: The company is best positioned as an asset-light platform and "marketplace connector" rather than a typical physical space provider. Business model described as: "If WeWork, Airbnb, and BNI had a baby." Could appeal to marketplace, community-oriented, or real estate tech investors.

- **Asset-Light Model and Scalability**: Spaces list with them like Airbnb — not renting or owning assets. Allows scaling up and down quickly. Mohamed Anis emphasized asset-light framing opens the investor pool significantly.

- **B2B Strategy and Community Work Days**: B2B strategy involves running community work days that lead individuals to become advocates, resulting in their companies seeking space. Utilizing empty/slow times in hotels and conference rooms. Recent success: 15-person company toured 3 spaces and is expected to take one for a few months.

- **Fundraising Strategy — Relationship-led Approach**: Two approaches exist: (1) intent-led — direct pitch, used only for companies in severe need; (2) relationship-led — preferred. Build ~600 investor connections over 3 months, never pitching — only connecting, engaging, nurturing. Get ~250-300 investors "warmed up." On day 1-10 of month 4: announce raising in 2 weeks, creating urgency from a warm base.

- **Three-Month Commitment**: Mohamed Anis strongly advised against only trying 1 month — nothing compounding happens. Work in month 1 is X, month 2 is 2X, month 3 is 5X, number of responses in month 3 is 10x month 1. Same price charged every month.

- **Investor Targeting**: US-based, high net worth angel investors who have previously invested in related/adjacent industries. Mohamed Anis recommended transitioning LinkedIn profile from client-facing to investor-facing.

- **CRM and Service**: Dedicated investor CRM included in $400/month. Full access and transparency — client can see every message, like, and comment. Weekly meetings with the team (Bombay and Farah). Constant WhatsApp updates.

- **Timing**: Jason thinks timing is right — updating pitch deck, expecting hard traction with recurring revenue by mid-August. Strategy: start relationship building now, so by September investors are warm and ready.

- **Future GTM Services**: Fundraising is a trust-builder. Primary revenue stream is go-to-market services ($400/month per engine). Clients that do fundraising often become long-term GTM clients.
