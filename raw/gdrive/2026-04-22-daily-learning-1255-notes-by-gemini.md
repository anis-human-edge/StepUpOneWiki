### Details

- **86 Lost Opportunities**: Farah Ibrahim identified 86 lost opportunities after removing currently active clients (e.g., Disney, Brandon Smith) from the list. Target: win back over 50%.

- **Two Categories of Lost Clients**: (1) Lost before AI/CRM implementation — easier to win back by showing new personalization capabilities. (2) Lost after AI/CRM was in place — win back by demonstrating how the CRM is used for current clients.

- **Channel Priority — WhatsApp First**: Email was initially proposed as the primary channel. After team discussion and Anis critique, the correct sequence was established: WhatsApp → LinkedIn → Email. Use the available channel; only fall back if the primary channel isn't available.

- **Angle of Attack**: Research goal is to find a single natural conversation hook — something about the client's recent activity, product launch, or news — that enables a non-pitching first message. Avoid immediately asking for a meeting.

- **Multiple Contacts per Company**: Target 6-7 people at a company like Quench, 15 at Fujitsu. Include people who have moved to new companies.

- **3-Message Sequence**: Minimum 3 messages for non-responsive clients. First message: natural conversation/rapport (no meeting ask). Second and third: lightweight follow-ups with potentially different angles.

- **AI Agent System**: The dashboard shows the company structured as AI agents. The Farah agent is Head of System 4. When given a research task, it autonomously hired 3 specialist sub-agents and created subtasks. Mohamed Anis noted cost concerns but described it as "creating a large, skilled company overnight."

- **Social Media Nurturing**: Liking and commenting on lost clients' LinkedIn posts is a powerful reconnection strategy but should happen over months to feel natural, not immediately before outreach.
