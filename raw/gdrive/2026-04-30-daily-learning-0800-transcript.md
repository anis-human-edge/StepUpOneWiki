## Session Content

### Sales Navigator Segmentation

Mohamed Anis conducted a live session on segmenting large Sales Navigator lists (12K–18K leads) into manageable chunks of ~2,500. Key lesson: when you have a large list, cut it in half using big attributes first (company headcount, geography, company type). Don't try to cut it into small pieces — cut in half, then in half again.

- Company headcount is the most reliable filter because it gives accurate numbers (unlike geography which shows wrong counts).
- AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) were tested to see if they understood "strategic segmentation" vs. mathematical division. Gemini performed best.
- The "meat-cutting" metaphor: divide 100kg into six parts by cutting big first (50/50, then 25/25), not by cutting 1g at a time.

### LinkedIn Content and Image Quality

Mohamed Anis shared observations on LinkedIn post quality:
- ChatGPT's new image generation (Imagen 2.0) is the best available tool for creating LinkedIn visuals.
- Prompt engineering for images: specify size (portrait, 4:5 ratio is best for LinkedIn), ask for clean/simple style, iterate.
- A post got 26,000 impressions — attributed to engagement and comments, not image quality alone.
- First comment within 60 minutes is critical for triggering the viral loop. Responding to comments within 5–10 minutes keeps it going.
- Team discussion: Wenzi and Dar Salah were not performing on Anis's LinkedIn and Twitter accounts; they may be moved to less critical accounts (Facebook/Instagram).

### LinkedIn Profile Alignment for Outreach

When reaching out to fractional executives, the LinkedIn profile must match the message. AHMED FARHAN tested outreach and received feedback: "Your profile doesn't match what you're saying." Profile should say "raise capital and scale" to match GTM outreach to fractional executives.

A LinkedIn slideshow ("Create a slideshow") feature requires LinkedIn Premium. Shared premium accounts don't have this feature.

### Infrastructure and Fundraising

Mohamed Anis announced plans to raise funds specifically for infrastructure — AI tools, Wi-Fi, gadgets — rather than for charity. He distinguished infrastructure fundraising from charity: "it's okay to build infrastructure with the money we raise."

### Story: Alexander the Great and Integrity

Anis shared a story (attributed to Alexander the Great's biography) about two Indian men disputing ownership of gold found on land. Each refused to take it. Alexander was shocked — he expected greed but found integrity. The lesson: earn with your own hands. Referenced Abraham Lincoln's letter to his son's teacher: "$1 earned by his own hard work is better than $100 given by his father."
