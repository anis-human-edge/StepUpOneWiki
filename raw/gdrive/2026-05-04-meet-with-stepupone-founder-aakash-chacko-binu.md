## Details

This meeting is about a music-based learning game project Aakash Chacko Binu is building.

- **Game Mechanics:** When a player picks a "weapon" (called an "astra"), the system displays a sequence of musical scale notes (e.g., 1 3 1 3 1). The player must sing those notes in the correct pitch and within a time limit for the weapon to fire. If pitch is wrong or time runs out, the player loses their turn and takes damage.

- **Musical Scale Components:** Numbers 1–7 correspond to the seven notes of the musical scale. The game also uses a version with "Sarama commands" (Sa Re Ga Ma notation) for Indian musicians.

- **Scaling Content (Astras):** An infinite number of astras can be created by varying variables: tempo, rhythm, complexity of notes, specific ragas (thousands exist), rhythm cycles. The system already generates astras algorithmically — they are not pre-fed manually.

- **Difficulty Progression:** Simple exercises involve 3–4 notes. The most advanced astra (Brahmastra) includes 10 notes with tricky sharps and flats requiring years of musical training. Players must step up to higher-level astras to win, creating an incentive for learning.
