### Summary

Session efficiency problems and beginner support strategies were discussed, with a major focus on defining inbound versus outbound strategies and planning the research engine for client engagement.  
  
**Improve Session Efficiency**  
Excessive time consumption due to assisting beginners was identified as a major issue, leading to the decision to move beginner support into a short, separate, dedicated time frame to maintain efficiency. The meeting emphasized that teaching fundamental concepts during the main session is counterproductive.  
  
**Define Inbound and Outbound**  
The difference between inbound and outbound was clarified: outbound involves the team actively chasing clients, and inbound involves the team creating content to make clients pursue them. The major assignment was documenting the core logic and architecture of the entire client outbound system for managed agents.  
  
**Structure Inbound Strategy Portfolio**  
Inbound must be structured as a portfolio of parallel strategies—such as commenting, posting, and blogging—instead of a simplified, sequential structure like outbound. The core insight is that the value of each inbound strategy is determined by the quality of the research, which involves tracking 100 to 300 segmented accounts and identifying 20 to 30 comment opportunities daily.
