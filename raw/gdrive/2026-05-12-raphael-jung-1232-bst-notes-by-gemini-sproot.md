### Details

- **Discussion on Education and AI's Impact**: Raphael Jung questioned the relevance of traditional university paths in the age of AI, wondering if hands-on skills might be more valuable. Mohamed Anis focused on the necessity of teaching critical thinking, even to 10-year-olds, as a tool to evaluate information from sources like AI. Critical thinking is not about increasing intelligence but about preparing individuals to face "fake news" or scams.
- **Mohamed Anis's Approach to Critical Thinking Education**: Mohamed Anis explained they are developing a structured toolkit to teach critical thinking for 10-year-olds, which involves asking the five W's (who, what, when, where, why) and understanding mechanisms behind the scenes. They decided to solve for critical thinking first, recognizing that it is a skill needed by all people. Mohamed Anis intends to make this educational content available for free.
- **Spruce's Funding Status and Interest in AI Support**: Raphael Jung confirmed that Sprout/Sproot are cash flow positive and progressing nicely, but would still be interested in finding suitable investors. They discussed a previous suggestion from Mo regarding using AI to contact potential investors.
- **AI's Role in Investor Research and Outreach Strategy**: Mohamed Anis explained that their core thesis is "human plus AI equal to outcomes" and that they use AI for research, creating personalized outreach messages, and gathering intelligence on investor theses and investment history. They explicitly stated they do not use AI to automate any of the outreach. They are focused on building relationships rather than pitching immediately, connecting with investors, engaging with their posts, and nurturing the connection for about three months before raising capital.
- **Investor Targeting and Ticket Size Focus**: Mohamed Anis shared that their list includes about 1,000 global high-net-worth angel investors who have invested in agriculture within the last 12 months. The target ticket size for a $2 million SAFE raise is typically between $200,000 to $500,000 from these angels.
- **Focusing on Investor Relationship Building**: The strategy involves highly personalizing connections with the identified 1,000 investors, aiming for 400 to 500 lifelong connections on LinkedIn. Mohamed Anis noted that pitching on the first message usually results in losing a lot of people; therefore, they prefer building relationships with 500 investors over time. No pitching for a good 3 months — just connect, engage, nurture, comment on posts.
- **Geographic Focus and Investor Feedback**: Mohamed Anis mentioned that while their list has 1,000 investors globally, less than 200 would likely be from Europe, and fewer than 100 have invested in Africa. Mohamed Anis suggested focusing on US investors first to get direct, honest feedback and potentially a lead investor before attacking the European market.
- **Service Pricing**: $400/month per person. Go-to-market service (post-funding) is where serious money is made — e.g., one startup grew to $100M revenue in New York with 50 people working in go-to-market.
- **Go-to-Market Strategy**: Put a team of around 10 people across 10 countries/ICPs. Run 10 experiments in month 1, identify the best-performing ICP, then focus all 10 on that single ICP from month 2. Pricing is $400/month/person.
- **Request for Investor List Sample**: Raphael Jung requested that Mohamed Anis share a few examples from their list of investors to assess potential overlap with Sproot's existing list of over a thousand contacts. Mohamed Anis agreed — Shermugget to create a CRM with the first 100 investors for review.
