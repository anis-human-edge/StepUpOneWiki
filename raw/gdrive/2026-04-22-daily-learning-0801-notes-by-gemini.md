### Details

- **Discussion on Building System 4: Win Back Lost Clients**: Farah Ibrahim initiated a discussion regarding the development of "System 4," focused on winning back lost clients, and requested team collaboration for this effort. They reported having identified 86 lost opportunities and expressed confidence that the team could win back a significant percentage of them, potentially over 50%. The system development will be cooperative, with Farah Ibrahim leading the process and expecting ideas and input from the rest of the team.

- **Categorization of Lost Clients**: The lost clients were divided into two categories based on the status of the company's internal systems at the time of loss. The first category includes clients lost when the company lacked an AI system, a working CRM, and an advanced messaging strategy. The second category includes clients lost more recently, maybe six or seven months ago, when the company already had an AI system in place.

- **Strategy for Winning Back Clients Based on Categorization**: Farah Ibrahim suggested that the clients lost before the implementation of AI and CRM would be relatively easier to win back by showcasing the new personalization capabilities provided by the AI. For clients lost more recently (when AI was already in place), the strategy involves using the CRM to attract them by demonstrating how it is used for current clients.

- **Identifying and Removing Currently Active Clients**: A concern was raised by AHMED FARHAN about currently active clients (like Disney and Brandon Smith) being included in the list of lost opportunities. Farah Ibrahim stated that they had previously shared instructions in the team leaders group to mark such clients, and requested that team members remove or inform them of any currently working clients identified in the win-back opportunity list.

- **Initial Touchpoint Strategy: Email Outreach**: Farah Ibrahim proposed that the best initial "touch point" for the lost clients is a short email, since a previous relationship already exists. Two draft email options were shared, both focusing on the time elapsed since the last contact, expressing appreciation for past work, and highlighting new innovations for delivering strong outcomes. The goal of the email is to secure a quick catch-up call, not to pitch services.

- **Initial Critique of System 4 by Mohamed Anis**: Mohamed Anis questioned whether the proposed system, which initially consisted of finding emails and sending a booking link, represented the full extent of the team's capacity for winning back clients, emphasizing the need for a comprehensive system. Farah Ibrahim clarified that the email and catch-up call were only the first stages, followed by proposal writing and the actual pitch. Mohamed Anis advised that the system should be written down clearly so that it can be reviewed and improved.

- **Team Contribution and Feedback on the Draft System**: Mohamed Anis pointed out that the current draft system places a high strain on their time and involves "zero value add" from the team members, as the system does not require their direct participation until the proposal writing stage. The team's feedback was criticized for being too basic, with Mohamed Anis expressing disappointment that team members did not suggest alternative communication channels, such as WhatsApp, before email.

- **Alternative Channel Strategy: WhatsApp and LinkedIn**: The team ultimately concluded that email should be the third channel for outreach. The correct sequence of channels for attack should prioritize WhatsApp first, followed by LinkedIn, and finally email, in cases where the primary channels are unavailable.

- **Social Media Engagement as a Nurturing Strategy**: A suggestion was made to proactively engage with clients on LinkedIn by liking and commenting on their recent posts to create a reason for speaking to them. While this "nurturing" action is seen as a very powerful way to re-establish connections, it was noted that it should occur over months to appear natural, not immediately before sending an email.

- **Research Scope and the "Angle of Attack"**: Farah Ibrahim asked how deep the research on the 100 lost clients should go before the catch-up call, to avoid the temptation of creating a pitch prematurely. Mohamed Anis clarified that the research's goal is to identify a singular "hook" or "angle of attack" — a natural, non-pitching conversation point about the client's recent activities, products, or changes. This attack should be done by the person connected to the client, but the research and learning should happen at the team member and team leader level.

- **System Components and Multi-Channel Attack Strategy**: Mohamed Anis outlined the key components of a robust system: angle of attack (a natural conversation hook), channel of attack (WhatsApp, LinkedIn, Email), and people to attack. The attack should target not just one contact, but multiple people within a former client company (e.g., 6-7 people at Quench, 15 people at Fujitsu), including those who have moved to new companies.

- **Multiple Message Sequence Requirement**: The strategy must incorporate a minimum of three message sequences for non-responsive clients, with the second and third messages being lightweight follow-ups, which can also incorporate different "angles." The first message should focus on natural conversation and building rapport, not immediately asking for a meeting.

- **Introducing the AI Organizational Structure Dashboard**: Mohamed Anis presented an organizational chart-like visualization, which was revealed to be a dashboard showing different "systems" or AI agents (e.g., AHMED FARHAN's System 1). This visualization details the agent's instructions, skills, capabilities, and budget.

- **Clarification of Company Goals and Systems**: Mohamed Anis clarified the structure and goals of the "step up one" project, emphasizing the three primary objectives: building the number one AI-native company to $1 million MR in 12 months, reskilling and deploying at least 2,000 operators in the next 12 months, and owning the thought leadership category of "human + AI = outcomes." Achieving the revenue goal necessitates building five specific systems, two of which (System 1 and System 4) are currently active.

- **Introduction of AI Agents and Imitation**: The group discussed the purpose of the new system, which includes components for goals, systems (1-5), and "agents" (like CEO, inbound, and specific people such as Farah). Mohamed Anis revealed that the system is designed to be a company where no humans will work, as it features an AI version of the employees that will imitate them, learn their skills, and perform tasks such as posting and writing.

- **Demonstration of AI Agent Task Assignment and Hiring**: Mohamed Anis demonstrated the capability of the AI system by assigning a task to Farah's agent (Head of System 4, responsible for winning back lost clients) that required researching the best angle of attack to win back lost clients. This process resulted in the system automatically hiring, training, and assigning three subordinate AI people (a LinkedIn analyst, a social analyst, and a company analyst) to assist the Farah agent with the research task.

- **Conclusion and Future Plans**: The system successfully created subtasks for the hired AI team and began running the assignment, demonstrating the immediate capability to manage complex tasks and resources. Mohamed Anis noted they were worried about the cost implications but recognized the immense power of the system, which allows for the literal creation of a large, skilled company overnight. They concluded the meeting by committing to running the system and human teams in parallel.
