Meeting with Wasim (Farook) and Jason from Gen Z Protect to review the existing proposal and discuss needed updates to the funnel architecture and dealer qualification flow.

### Details

- **$840K/Year Profit Potential**: Wasim and Jason want the dealer-facing materials to lead with the quantified opportunity — showing how much additional profit a dealership could earn per year by adopting the Gen Z Protect product. This figure ($840K/year) must be prominent early in the funnel.

- **Facebook Groups as Primary Target**: The primary channel for hook content is Facebook groups for car dealership owners and finance directors. Posts, polls, and comments in these groups serve as the top-of-funnel entry point.

- **Funnel Flow (Revised)**: Hook (paid ads / organic posts / polls / comments) → multi-step qualification gate (4-5 questions) → personalized summary report showing potential savings/gains → booking link OR "learn more" (which goes to the original landing page). The original landing page is now designated as a "learn more" destination, not the primary CTA.

- **CTA Debate — "Join Now" vs Booking**: There was debate between using a "join now" button (immediate commitment) versus a booking link (softer step). No final decision recorded — to be resolved in the addendum.

- **Addendum Web Page**: A new web page addendum will be built and placed next to the existing proposal page to show the updated funnel architecture clearly. This addendum is the immediate deliverable.

- **Hook Examples Required**: Once the addendum is complete, the team must produce 3-4 example versions of each hook type: posts, polls, blog ideas, and comment ideas targeting Facebook dealership groups.

- **Qualification Gate and Report**: The qualification gate has 4-5 steps. After completion, the dealer sees a personalized report showing their potential uplift/savings. This report is what motivates the booking action.
