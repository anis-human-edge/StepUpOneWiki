## Details

### Plan B Business Model

Chris Thomas introduced "Plan B" — generating easy short-term money by targeting multiple businesses across different industries that share common challenges:
- Weak shop front (website/digital presence)
- Weak back end
- Need for more customers

The strategy: approach these businesses offering to sell them a pre-built shop front, or offer to run it for them to bring in leads.

**Visual proof points are critical:** Simply proposing improvements is ineffective. A visual reference (even a screenshot, not necessarily a fully functioning interface) dramatically increases conversion rates. Clients can immediately see the improved interface.

**Build timeline:**
- Visual reference only: ~1 day
- Complete working version: ~7 days
- The build requires a 2-hour rich conversation with the client beforehand to gather input (what they tried, how they failed, what problems exist). The actual build from that input takes ~1 hour.

**Pricing:**
- Initial build fee: 5K–10K for the conversion engines (inbound + outbound)
- Monthly fee: ~5K/month for running the engines and delivering leads
- Profit margin: ~30–40% on the monthly run (not much profit on the initial build)

### Visual Sales Approach

Mohamed Anis referenced the Gen Z Protect (car dealership) proposal, which evolved from a simple proposal into an "execution playbook" — a visual document showing:
- The full solution from hooks to the qualification engine
- Live examples: posts, polls, amplification comments, blog briefs, paid ads, group amplification
- The 5-stage funnel: Hook → Qualify → Report → Landing Page → Book
- Outcome: 1,000 clients per month through the stages, with 5–10 fully qualified meetings delivered

A referral from the Pergola client resulted in a new prospect who reviewed the proposal independently (copied from another client, no personalization) and expressed strong interest — demonstrating the proposal's self-selling power.

### Outreach Strategy for Plan B

Chris Thomas proposed:
1. Select a few anchor verticals (businesses that are good at their product/service but hate the marketing)
2. Communicate to them: "A competitor in your industry recently engaged us. We're not going to show you everything yet (NDA), but here are screenshots. Interested in talking?"
3. If they bite: onboarding process (5 questions or a video, no need for a call initially)
4. Use the CRM to manage communications

### CRM Architecture Deep Dive

Mohamed Anis walked Chris Thomas through the CRM (GoHighLevel-based):
- **Core:** Contacts (100K potential clients in smart lists — angel investors, PE firms, stealth founders, Y Combinator founders, TechStar founders, etc.)
- **Workflow:** Contacts are nurtured on LinkedIn first. When they start communicating, they become "Opportunities."
- **Channels:** Email, SMS, WhatsApp can all be sent from the CRM
- **Smart lists:** Created using filters (e.g., "funding stage = X") to segment and send targeted messages
- **Custom fields:** Can be added to capture any lead data — revenue, sector, source, EBITDA, etc. Simple to add via Settings → Custom Fields.
- **Inbound:** Forms on landing pages can be integrated (though tricky — CSV import is an easier workaround)
- **Calendar/scheduling:** Built into the CRM
