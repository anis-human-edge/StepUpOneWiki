### Summary

Societal impact of technology and AI curriculum for the underserved were explored with a critique of a proposed speaker refurbishment project.  
  
**Addressing Talent and Technology**  
The discussion established the goal of helping individuals discover talents rather than just providing money, noting challenges like maintaining motivation among trauma-affected participants. It was observed that superior technology utilized by the team is meaningless unless it yields real-world results for underserved populations.  
  
**AI Curriculum and Training**  
An overview of the offered AI curriculum was provided, emphasizing instruction in essential skills like creating websites and social media content, without contracts or fees. A trial of the AI instruction was proposed for an accessible group, specifically the post-release population Rivelino "Blackrockstar" Rigters works with, due to prison internet restrictions.  
  
**Critique of Speaker Project**  
The existing speaker refurbishment project was strongly advised against due to difficulty competing with mass production and low added value for the target population. It was suggested that AI-built technology must be combined with human talent to ensure people pay for tremendous value, and a "show and tell" of AI possibilities was recommended to inspire participants.
