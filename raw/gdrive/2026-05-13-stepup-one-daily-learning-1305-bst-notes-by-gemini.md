Abdulkadir Mhina accepted the correction and explained the next step is to paste the content into the "studio" tool, where the system automatically detects the post.
- **Function and Context of AI Detection in the Studio Tool**: Byaombe Wilondja asked for clarification on the "AI detected" section in the studio tool, which was showing categories like "executive talent" and "engineering leaders". Chol Akuien and Abdulkadir Mhina clarified that the AI detects the post's content and context, categorizing it based on its subject matter, such as talent and acquisition for engineering leaders. Chol Akuien also added that the content generated by the system will link directly to the relevant page.
- **Comment Generation, Review, and Traffic Driving Strategy**: Abdulkadir Mhina demonstrated that the commenting process involves selecting "one line" for the output, generating the comment, and then checking to ensure the comment aligns with the original post. The strategy for inbound traffic generation is to attract readers with a relevant comment, which includes a link that leads them to a landing page where they can book a call if they are convinced. Abdulkadir Mhina noted that not all ten comments will result in a conversion, but a few people may "dive inside the landing page."

Najibismail Hajir and Chol Akuien clarified that it is indirect selling because the comment first addresses the original post's context, and then the selling line is added as a related point, unlike direct selling, which would start with the sales pitch.
- **Best Practices for Post Selection and Engagement**: Abdulkadir Mhina emphasized that reading the post before commenting is crucial to ensure relevance and determine if the post will add value. Demonstrated that a highly engaged post that is three weeks old is considered "already dead" — advised following the author instead of commenting. The team selected a recent post (21 hours old with 572 engagements) for a final demonstration, though a network issue prevented the final generation. Rule: comment on posts less than 1 hour old.
- **Proposal for Using the Organizational Brand for Commenting**: Mohamed Anis proposed that the team transition from commenting using their personal profiles to using the organization's brand profiles on platforms like Facebook and Instagram to capitalize on the higher credibility of the brand (9,000 followers). Mohamed Anis suggested that they should comment as the "founder of Step Up One," speaking like a person, using "I" and "we," rather than as a faceless brand to maintain authenticity. Abdulkadir Mhina was tasked with experimenting on LinkedIn first, then distributing the work across the team for other channels once the method is proven successful.
- **Shifting Focus from Teaching to Outcome**: Mohamed Anis concluded by stressing that the "system five" objective needs to shift its focus from continually teaching the system to achieving measurable outcomes. Mohamed Anis highlighted that the system needs to be used to deliver impact, similar to the work of another team member (Ahmed Farhan), rather than repeating the same training sessions. "You're teaching really really well but you can give them a platform where they can practice what you're teaching."
